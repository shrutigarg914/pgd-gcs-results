Maintaining Feasibility Guarantees when Using Nonconvex Objectives in Graphs of Convex Sets for Trajectory Optimization

Shruti Garg, Thomas Cohn, Russ Tedrake

Optimization based motion planning provides a useful modeling framework through various costs and constraints. Using Graph of Convex Sets (GCS) for trajectory optimization gives guarantees of feasibility and optimality, by representing configuration space as the finite union of convex sets. Nonlinear parameterizations can be used to extend this technique to handle cases such as kinematic loops, but this warps distances, so solving with convex objectives will yield paths that are suboptimal in the original space. We present a method to extend GCS to non-convex objectives, allowing us to "undistort" the optimization landscape while maintaining feasibility guarantees. We demonstrate our methodâ€™s efficacy on three different robotic planning domains: a bimanual robot moving an object with both arms, the set of 3D rotations using Euler angles, and a rational parametrization of kinematics that enables certifying regions as collision free. Across the board, our method significantly improves path length and trajectory duration with only a minimal increase in runtime.
